Silver and Currencies

Protection in Uncertain Times

Why Silver?

A tangible store of value

Silver is the lesser-known precious metal. The best conductor of heat and electricity known to man, silver has 3,800 industrial uses and counting.

For decades the world has used more silver than it has mined each year. The resulting deficit could result in a price explosion in the not-too-distant future.

Silver is also much more useful than gold in barter in the event of a currency collapse. Most important, silver is an industrial and a precious metal in high demand and declining supply. It has investment potential unlike any other asset.

Governments cannot create more silver nor diminish its value.

Why Foreign Currencies?
