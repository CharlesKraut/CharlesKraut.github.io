How to Skydive

Skydiving is a pastime enjoyed by thousands of thrill-seekers.

It is a sport not particularly forgiving of mistakes or oversights.

To participate, some equipment is essential. The most important is probably the parachute.

Unless you are Superman, an airplane will also come in handy:

Got all that? Then let's go!

  1. Get your gear together.
  2. Pack your parachute.

    3. Never let someone else pack your parachute!

  3. Make sure you have adequate life insurance.
  4. Check to see that your will is up-to-date.
  5. Schedule your flight.

    6. This may be the only time you will purchase a half-way ticket.. Think about that..

  6. Follow all instructions from the jumpmaster.
  7. Familiarize yourself with the ripcords for the main and the reserve chutes.
  8. When the jumpmaster says "jump," make sure you jump out through the open door.

    9. Otherwise you may have to pay the full one-way fare.

  9. Once out of the plane, fight the temptation to panic. Your action is irreversible.
  10. It is advisable to pull the ripcord too early rather than too late. Better to miss the landing zone than to impact it at high speed.

    11. If the first ripcord fails, try to remember that you have another one.

  11. If the second ripcord fails to open a parachute, you won't be needing these instructions any more.

    12. Happy Landing!
